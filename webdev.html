Web Dev

As a former English major, who spent the majority of my academic history studying the liberal arts, I never envisioned I would one day consider myself a web designer or "programmer" in any capacity.

However, at my previous role, there came a point where our website was due for a major overhaul, and there was no one familiar with the platform it was hosted on (Craft CMS). Seeing an opportunity to learn new skills, I decided to rise to the task. It is here that I discovered a passion for web, and discovering the best ways to organize and display information. I took immense satisfaction in not only writing copy for web, but also designing the page around it. I learned a lot on the job, and loved every second of it.

Since leaving that position, I have spent my spare time self-studying the technical side of web development, including HTML, CSS, Git and Github. Below you can find a sample of my personal and professional work in this field.

Porfolio Website

My portfolio website, which you are currently viewing consists of HTML and CSS, and is currently hosted via Github pages. Previously, I had created a site using Wixsite, however, I wanted to put my skills to the test and create something entirely my own.

When designing the layout of the site, my goal was to strike a balance between professionality and personality. My font choice paired with white and light grey backgrounds are intended to give the site a clean feeling; the pops of green infuse it with a sense of liveliness.
When designing the landing page I was very mindful of the fact that it is the first thing any visitors will see. Included is a 3D model of my workspace which I created myself using Blender. I was excited to include this as it was a chance to utilize yet another skill which I have been learning on my own, while also showcasing a little bit about myself: my dog, my love of plants and my love of books. I also hoped that this would create a cozy environment to welcome visitors to my page with.

While the website is currently complete for viewing in a browser, there are many parts that are not yet optimized for mobile, which will be my next major project moving forward.
